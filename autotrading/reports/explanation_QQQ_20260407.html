2. Step 1: Pivot Detection

Pivots are the local turning points the engine uses as anchors for trendlines and support/resistance zones. The detection follows Grimes's three-order hierarchy — only first-order pivots (the most significant local extremes) are used here.

Detection Rules (15-min tier parameters)

  • Window: ±3 bars — a high is a pivot only if it is strictly greater than all bars within 3 bars on each side (similarly for lows).
  • ATR confirmation: The price swing from the previous pivot of the opposite type must be ≥ 0.75× ATR(14) = at least $1.431. This filters micro-wiggles that are not meaningful swings.
  • Alternation: Pivots must strictly alternate high → low → high → … The zigzag is enforced so consecutive same-side pivots are impossible.
  • Minimum separation: Same-side pivots must be ≥ 20 bars apart to prevent clusters of nearby pivots being treated as distinct turning points.
  • Intervening swing: The counterswing between two same-side pivots must be ≥ 1.0× ATR(14) ≈ $1.907 — ensures "open water" between pivots, ruling out minor retracements.
  • Channel span: The full set of anchors must span ≥ 80 bars so the trendline is not based on a tiny, unrepresentative window.

5. Step 4: Support & Resistance Zones

S/R zones are horizontal price bands where the market has repeatedly reversed. The engine clusters nearby pivot prices and scores each cluster on four dimensions:

Scoring Formula

  • Touch count × 2.0 — each time price tests the zone from above (resistance) or below (support) without decisively breaking through, the zone gains 2.0 points.
  • Recency × 1.5 — more recent touches score higher. Zones not retested within 400 bars have their score halved (time decay, per Bulkowski).
  • Volume at touches × 1.0 — high-volume reversals at the zone confirm it is a meaningful level (not thin-market noise).
  • Role reversal bonus × 3.0 — if the level previously acted as support and now acts as resistance (or vice versa), it scores an extra 3.0 points. Former support becomes resistance after a breakdown, and vice versa — a well-established concept in technical analysis.

Acceptance Criteria

  • Minimum score: ≥ 4.0
  • Minimum touches: ≥ 2
  • Zone clustering tolerance: 0.3% of price (or 0.5× ATR, whichever is larger) — pivots within this band are grouped into one zone.
  • Zones weaken by 10% per touch beyond the 3rd (0.90^(touches-2) multiplier) — repeated tests erode the zone's resistance to future breakouts.
